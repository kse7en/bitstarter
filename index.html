FAQ

Why a Housing Cooperative?
Housing cooperatives are a well known alternative ownership model. Housing Cooperatives are less expensive than other forms of housing. They operate on a at-cost basis, collecting money only to cover the cost.
How does shared ownership work?
Usually all property is owned by an organisation (the cooperative). Coop members are shareholders and often entitled to live in one of the units against rent or upfront payment.
Who is this for?
A Housing Cooperative is a well structured and regulated ownership model that is used all over the world, in cities as diverse as Tokyo and Toronto. The model works for hippies, hipsters, families and urbanites.
How does CoFindr help me?
CoFindr want's to connect people in the same area who are looking for a housing cooperative or want to start their own project. The most difficult part is often to find the right people. We are giving you the tools to make the promotion of your ideas easier. And our knowledge base and discussion forums can guide you through the process.
