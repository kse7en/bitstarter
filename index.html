FAQ

Why a Housing Cooperative?
Housing cooperatives are a well known alternative ownership model. Cooperatives are less expensive than other forms of housing, e.g.apartments, because they operate on an at-cost basis, collecting money from residents just to cover the cost. With rising cost of owning a home in most cities, this is an attractive option for many families.
What exactly is a Housing Cooperative?
In a Housing Cooperative the property is owned by an organization and then sold as shares to the residents of the community. As a shareholder, you are entitled to exclusive use of a housing unit in the property. Cooperative housing is not simply a purchase of real estate and a place to live: it also includes obligations and opportunity to involve yourself in the governance, organization and activities of the community.
Is this only for Hippies? How is this different from Communes?
Housing Cooperatives area well structured and regulated shared ownership model. They can be as close and as far away from traditional apartment living or a commune as you define it.
How does CoFindr help me?
CoFindr want's to connect people in the same area who are looking for a housing cooperative or want to start their own project. The most difficult part is often to find the right people. We are giving you the tools to make the promotion of your ideas easier. And our knowledge base and discussion forums can guide you through the process.
